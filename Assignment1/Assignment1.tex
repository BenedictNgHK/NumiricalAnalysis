\section*{Problem}
If the first 6 terms of Taylor expansion are used to calculate\newline
\begin{center}
\(\int_{0}^{1} e^{-x^2} \,dx\)
\end{center}
find its error, including truncation error and roundoff error.
\section*{Answer}
Let \(f(x)=e^{-x^2}\) and expand the original equation with Taylor Series at point 0.
\begin{flalign*}
\int_{0}^{1}f(x) &= \int_{0}^{1}\Sigma_{n = 0}^{\infty}\frac{f^{(n)}(0)}{n!}(x)^n &\\
                 &= \int_{0}^{1}\Sigma_{m = 0}^{\infty}\frac{{(-1)^m}x^{2m}}{m!}  &\\
                 &= \Sigma_{m = 0}^{m \to \infty}\frac{{(-1)^m}}{(2m+1) \cdot m!} &
\end{flalign*}
And its first 6 non-zero terms are:
\begin{flalign*}
\int_{0}^{1}f(x)    &\approx \int_{0}^{1} (1 - x^2 + \frac{x^4}{2!} - \frac{x^6}{3!} + \frac{x^8}{4!} - \frac{x^{10}}{5!})|_{0}^{1} &\\
                    &\approx  x - \frac{x^3}{3} + \frac{x^5}{5 \cdot 2!} - \frac{x^7}{7 \cdot 3!} + \frac{x^9}{9 \cdot 4!} - \frac{x^{11}}{11 \cdot 5!}|_{x=0}^{x=1} &\\
                    &\approx \Sigma_{m = 0}^{m = 5}\frac{{(-1)^m}}{(2m+1) \cdot m!} &
\end{flalign*}\newpage
